What are SDG's?

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are 17 interconnected global goals adopted by the United Nations in 2015. They were created to end poverty, protect the planet, and improve the lives of people everywhere by 2030.

These goals focus on important issues such as quality education, good health, gender equality, clean water, climate action, and reduced inequalities. Together, they provide a shared blueprint for a more peaceful and sustainable future.

How does it relate to our website?

In summary, Composting, electricity use, and BYOC (Bring Your Own Container) all connect strongly to the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) by promoting sustainability and responsible consumption.

Composting supports SDG 12 (Responsible Consumption and Production) and SDG 13 (Climate Action) by reducing food waste and lowering methane emissions from landfills. It also improves soil health, contributing to more sustainable agriculture.

electricity use in ISM aligns with SDG 7 (Affordable and Clean Energy) and SDG 13 (Climate Action) by encouraging sustainable energy production through renewable energy sources, And helping save the climate overall by releasing less greenhouse gasses.

Finally, BYOC directly supports SDG 12 by cutting down on single use plastic and also SDG 13 by reducing emmisions and plastic production and paper container production.
